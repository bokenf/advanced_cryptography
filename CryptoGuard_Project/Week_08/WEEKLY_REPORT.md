## 3. Student Reflection
This week, I focused on the trust mechanisms and mathematical foundations of modern digital security. I analyzed the Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) framework, detailing how Certificate Authorities (CAs) and digital certificates establish identity and trust. Furthermore, I explored asymmetric cryptography and the Diffie-Hellman protocol, learning how mathematical intractability—specifically the Discrete Logarithm Problem—allows two parties to securely establish shared secrets over insecure networks. By implementing the Diffie-Hellman exchange in Python and inspecting X.509 certificates via OpenSSL, I gained a practical understanding of how theoretical complexity protects real-world communications.
